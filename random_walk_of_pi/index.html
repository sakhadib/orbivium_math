What and How !?

What is a Random Walk?

A random walk is a mathematical concept that describes a path consisting of a sequence of random steps. It is widely used in physics, biology, economics, and other fields to model phenomena like stock market movements, particle diffusion, and more. In this visualizer, each step corresponds to a digit of Pi, with its angle and direction determined by the digit value.

How is Pi Related to Random Walks?

Pi is an irrational number with an infinite, non-repeating sequence of decimal digits. Using these digits, we can assign each digit an angle and a corresponding step in a random walk. This makes Pi's digits an excellent source of randomness for generating unique, unpredictable paths.

Why Does the Graph Differ for Input 70 vs. 200?

The difference in the graph's shape is due to the number of steps taken. With an input of 70, the walk has fewer steps, resulting in a simpler, less complex graph. In contrast, with an input of 200, the additional steps introduce more intersections, loops, and overall complexity, leading to a denser, more intricate visualization.

Additional Insights

- The visualization is deterministic, meaning it will always produce the same graph for a given input.
- You can explore the walk interactively by panning and zooming to better understand the structure of the graph.
